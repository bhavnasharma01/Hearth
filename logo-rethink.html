Logo rethink · round five · July 11, 2026

The flame, not the drop

A member read the flame cutout in our heart as a drop of blood. Looking at the geometry, they have a point: the current cutout is a perfectly symmetric teardrop, and symmetry is what makes droplets read as droplets. Real flames are asymmetric: they lean, flick, and split into tongues. So the fix may not be abandoning the heart at all, just teaching the cutout to actually burn. Every candidate below is shown on both stages, in the header at true size (34px), and at favicon sizes, because that is where marks live or die.

Notes from the exploration

A fireplace surround was tried and set aside: drawing the heart as an outline (the hearth frame) with a flame and mantel bar inside got busy fast. At 34px the three elements fight, and the stroked heart plus bar made an odd double-chin at the bottom point. The hearth-bar idea survives in candidate C in a calmer form.
On bringing the old flame back (candidate G): it is a lovely mark, but it was retired for a reason that has not changed: the other Hearth app (hearthapp.ca) wears a warm standalone flame, and ours would sit a coin-flip away from it. The heart is our differentiation, and the heart·hearth·earth wordplay is the brand story. Recommendation: keep the heart, fix the flame inside it.
Shipping whichever wins is one small build: the mark lives in four places (logo.tsx, logo.svg, email-logo.png, the favicon icon.svg), all updated together. Nothing else in the identity moves: palette, type, and layout stay exactly as they are.
