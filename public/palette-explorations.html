Hearth — palette explorations

Start with the five palette finalists and the five logo & favicon directions below — built for deciding together (each logo is shown as a favicon tile in the finalist palettes). The 60 exploration cards that led here follow after.

Any finalist can be worn by the whole live site within minutes — pick two, wear each for a day, and the decision makes itself.

The finalists

The lesson of 60 cards: the strong-looking palettes paint the big surfaces dark (heavy), the gentle ones fade away (too light). The way out, used by the best consumer apps: a near-neutral canvas, dark warm ink, and ONE confident accent doing the identity work — in the app, the email, and the favicon. Five ways to do that, each shown as an app screen and a notification email.

Logo & favicon directions

The other Hearth owns "a flame silhouette" — so these five own something richer. Judge each three ways: does it still read at favicon size (the small tiles, shown in finalist palettes F1 / F3 / F4)? does it say hearth? is it clearly not their mark? Recommendation: L1 the Hearth arch (the fireplace itself — the most "hearth" and the most ownable), with L3 the Gathering as the community-first alternative.

L1

The Hearth arch

A fireplace opening with a small fire inside — the literal hearth, and the warm front door.

Hearth
as favicon in F1 · F3 · F4

Why it's the recommendation: nobody else's flame comes with a fireplace around it — instantly distinct, still warm, and the arch doubles as a doorway (the "warm front door" from the product's own North Star). Geometrically simple, so it stays crisp at 16px.

L2

The heart-flame

A heart whose centre holds a flame — the wordplay made visible (heart · earth · hearth).

Hearth
as favicon in F1 · F3 · F4

The most emotional option — it carries the founding conversation (Curtis spotting heart + earth in the name). Risk: hearts are common in wellness branding; ours earns its place only through the flame cutout, so it must never be simplified to a plain heart.

L3

The Gathering

A circle of people around a fire, abstracted to six points and a flame. What Hearth actually is.

Hearth
as favicon in F1 · F3 · F4

The community-first alternative — says "gathering place" louder than any flame could, and no competitor in this space uses it. Risk: at 16px the dots get small; it needs the favicon drawn slightly bolder than the display version.

L4

Embers rising

A hearthstone bowl with sparks lifting off — warmth in motion, hope, things beginning.

Hearth
as favicon in F1 · F3 · F4

The most modern of the five — geometric, calm, ages well. Reads beautifully as a favicon (big simple shapes). Risk: the least literal; some people will see a sunrise or a cauldron before they see embers — which may be fine.

L5

The lantern

A lantern with a small flame — carried warmth, and the act of finding someone.

Hearth
as favicon in F1 · F3 · F4

The seeker's mark — a lantern is what you carry when looking for someone, which is literally the directory's job. Distinct and friendly. Risk: lanterns lean "cottage/camping" for some viewers, and the fine strokes need thickening at favicon size.
